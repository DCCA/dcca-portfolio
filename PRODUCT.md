# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary visitor is a hiring manager, recruiter, or hiring team evaluating Daniel Andrade for a senior or lead AI product role (Group PM, Head of Product, or similar). They arrive skeptical and time-poor, doing a credibility check before deciding whether a conversation is worth their time. A strong secondary visitor is a founder or team weighing Daniel for advisory, fractional, or consulting work on an AI product. Both share the same question: does this person have real product judgment, and can he actually build?

Success is the visitor leaving convinced enough to connect on LinkedIn (primary) or dig into the shipped work on GitHub (secondary), carrying away a clear, specific impression of what Daniel does and why it is credible.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that establishes Daniel Andrade as a fintech product operator who builds AI-native product systems people can actually operate, review, and trust. It exists to convert a cold, skeptical visitor into a warm contact by proving judgment with shipped work rather than asserting it with adjectives. Success looks like the right people (senior AI product hiring, serious advisory conversations) reaching out already believing he is worth their time.

## Positioning

AI systems with product judgment. Daniel pairs senior fintech product leadership with hands-on building, and applies that judgment to the parts of AI that are hard to fake: signal curation, eval gates, and human-in-the-loop review. Every screen should reinforce that he ships AI you can actually trust, not demos.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Connect on LinkedIn. Secondary CTA: explore the shipped work / GitHub. Email is the direct fallback for a real conversation.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: a fintech operator who builds AI systems you can actually operate, review, and trust.
- Belief ladder (what the visitor must believe, in order, before connecting): (1) this person ships real AI systems, not demos — there is inspectable, working proof; (2) he has senior product judgment, earned as a fintech operator at known companies; (3) he works on the hard, trustworthy part of AI — evals, receipts, human review — not just the impressive surface; (4) staying connected to him is worth doing now.
- Proof on hand: four shipped systems (AI Signal Desk, skval, vyno, shotback) with public repos, plus an operator track record (Neon as Group PM / Sr. Manager; prior rooms at Mercado Libre, Leve, PagSeguro PagBank, ConectCar). No testimonials or hard metrics supplied yet; the work and the track record carry the proof.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, credible, understated. The voice is an experienced operator who has nothing to prove and therefore does not oversell: precise, evidence-first, confident in its restraint. It states the useful cut and the tradeoff, names what someone can challenge, and lets the work speak. Warmth comes through clarity and respect for the reader's time, not through hype, exclamation, or manufactured enthusiasm.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-slop SaaS: cream/beige backgrounds, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows over every section, gradient-accent hero-metric blocks, endless identical icon cards. The 2026 default that reads as "AI made this."
- Loud dev-portfolio gimmicks: neon-on-black, terminal cosplay, animated blobs, walls of skill badges, gratuitous scroll effects. Style substituting for substance.
- Corporate / stiff resume site: LinkedIn-beige, buzzword soup, stock-photo professionalism, no point of view. Safe and forgettable.
- Over-designed agency showreel: maximalist motion and oversized type for their own sake, form winning over legibility.

The site should be distinctive and opinionated without being loud — restraint with a POV, not restraint as an excuse for generic.

## Design Principles

- Show, don't claim. Every assertion is backed by a shipped system, a repo, or a receipt the reader can inspect. No adjective stands alone.
- Trusted operation beats impressive output. The design mirrors the thesis: legible, inspectable, honest about tradeoffs — the opposite of a demo.
- Small enough to inspect, real enough to validate. Favor concrete, verifiable proof over breadth; a few things that hold up beat many that don't.
- Restraint with a point of view. Understated is a deliberate stance, not a lack of one. Distinctiveness comes from precision and editorial judgment, not volume.
- Operator and builder in one person. The rare combination — senior product judgment plus hands-on code — is the throughline every section reinforces.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (large text ≥3:1), full keyboard navigation with visible focus, semantic landmarks and heading order, and honored `prefers-reduced-motion` on every animation. Motion stays purposeful and minimal so the site is comfortable for low-stimulation and reduced-motion visitors.
